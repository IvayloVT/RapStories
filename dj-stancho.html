Biography

The Architect of the Underground

The journey began in the twilight of the socialist era, when hip-hop in Bulgaria was still at what many would describe as level zero. At only 14 years old, Stanislav Peev was already moving like a pioneer. Together with Petar Somov, he co-founded Break Group Dynamic, one of the first serious attempts to introduce breakdance culture to a Bulgarian audience that had barely encountered it before.

At a time when Western youth culture still felt distant and half-mythical, Stancho and his circle did more than imitate what they had seen from abroad. They translated it into a local language, local movement, and local identity. Their choreography reached national television, making them among the first public faces of hip-hop expression in Bulgaria. Through projects such as Hip-Hop: The Fashionable Dance and the provocatively titled I’ll Swap Your Suit for a Wooden One, Stancho helped lead a transition: people stopped being curious spectators and started becoming participants in a new underground culture.

The Birth of an Empire: Sniper Records

As the 1990s unfolded, Stancho evolved beyond dance and into production, artist development, and cultural strategy. He founded Sniper Records, a label that would become one of the defining homes of real Bulgarian rap. In a period when the market often leaned toward lighter rap-dance formulas, Stancho pushed in the opposite direction. He wanted something heavier, grittier, and more authentic — music rooted in lyrical credibility rather than novelty.

That vision gave Sniper Records an identity far bigger than a normal label. It became a headquarters for an entire philosophy of hip-hop. Stancho stood behind some of the most legendary names associated with the movement, especially Spens and Slim, helping shape the sound and image of an era. Under his leadership, Sniper turned into a cultural power center. It launched the magazine Knockout, strengthened the intellectual side of the scene, and even built international bridges by bringing members of the Wu-Tang Clan to Bulgarian soil. That kind of ambition showed that Stancho was not just building artists — he was building infrastructure.

The Great Divide: The War of the Labels

By the early 2000s, DJ Stancho found himself at the center of the most explosive rivalry in Bulgarian hip-hop history: the battle between Sofia and Varna, between Sniper Records and Misho Shamara’s RNB Records. On one side stood Stancho’s camp — the Sofia underground, rooted in hardcore values, lyrical seriousness, and street authenticity. On the other stood the more commercial and flamboyant G-Funk spirit of RNB Records, a movement built around visibility, popularity, and chart dominance.

This was never just about business. It was a cultural civil war inside Bulgarian rap. The conflict produced diss tracks, divided audiences, and forced listeners to choose not just music, but a worldview. One side represented underground discipline and resistance to compromise; the other represented a louder, more market-facing version of rap success. While RNB often owned the clubs and radio moments, Stancho remained the steadfast general of the Sofia underground, refusing to water down the culture for easier consumption.

The Legacy

DJ Stancho’s legacy is inseparable from the story of how hip-hop took root in Bulgaria. From the earliest B-boy era in a country with barely any spaces for the culture, to the creation of one of its most important labels, his path reflects institution-building as much as artistry. He helped transform hip-hop from an imported curiosity into something lived, argued over, defended, and believed in locally.

That is why Stancho remains such a powerful symbol. He represents a period when Bulgarian rap was driven by message, conflict, identity, and hunger. He stands for the belief that even when a culture begins at level zero, one determined figure can create structure, movement, and mythology around it. Today, DJ Stancho remains one of the clearest emblems of the era when Bulgarian hip-hop had a raw soul, a distinct voice, and a true Sniper aim.

“Even at level zero, a single person with vision can build an entire world.”

Career Timeline

At 14, Stancho co-founded Break Group Dynamic with Petar Somov and helped introduce early breakdance culture to Bulgaria.

National Exposure — Early Hip-Hop Projects

Brought hip-hop choreography to national television and helped projects like Hip-Hop: The Fashionable Dance turn curiosity into participation.

1990s Expansion — Sniper Records

Founded Sniper Records and pushed Bulgarian rap toward a harder, more authentic underground identity.

Scene Leadership — Spens, Slim, and Knockout

Helped shape key artists, launched the Knockout magazine, and expanded the culture beyond music alone.

Early 2000s — The Battle of the Cities

Became the central Sofia figure in the legendary rivalry with RNB Records, defending underground values against commercial drift.

Legacy Era — The Godfather

Remains remembered as one of the core architects of Bulgarian hip-hop and a symbol of the scene’s rawest era.
